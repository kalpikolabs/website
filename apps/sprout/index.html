Sprout

Scan any plant, get its whole story.

Coming soon

Sprout identifies houseplants from a single photo and hands you a beautiful, screenshot-worthy care card — light, water, pet-safety, and what each plant loves and hates. Then it sets gentle, anti-shame watering reminders, per plant — “a little thirsty 💧”, never “OVERDUE”.

It’s made for anyone handed a plant who doesn’t know what to do with it. The first scan is free — no login, no paywall before value. A Discover tab lets you browse curated lists (beginner, pet-safe, low-light, flowering) without scanning. Everything stays on your device, and the single photo used for identification is never stored or linked to you.

A look inside

Good to know

Do I need an account?

No — Sprout works with zero login.

Where do my photos go?

They stay on your device; only the one identification photo is sent to the plant-ID service and isn’t stored.

Why did it call my fake plant real?

A photo can’t always tell a good faux plant from a real one — tap the Real/Faux toggle on the card and we’ll skip watering reminders for décor.

Is the first scan really free?

Yes, always.

A note on limits: artificial-plant detection from a single photo is imperfect — the Real/Faux toggle is the reliable control. Identification accuracy varies with photo quality and uncommon species, and reminders are local notifications (no cloud sync) in this first version.
