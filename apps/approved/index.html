Approved

Passport photos that pass — before you pay.

Coming soon

Approved turns a phone selfie into a compliance-checked passport, visa, or ID photo — sized to a chosen country’s published specs — and gives you both a digital file and a printable sheet, in under two minutes.

It’s for anyone facing a document deadline: travelers, students, immigrants, job applicants, and parents doing the whole family’s photos. Every requirement is shown as a plain-language pass or flag with how to fix it, so you know your photo will pass before you pay. Photos and results stay on your device; each photo is sent once to an AI service for the check and nothing is retained beyond the provider’s short window. And the pricing is honest and one-time — pay per photo or a 10-photo family pack, no subscription, ever.

A look inside

Good to know

Will my photo definitely be accepted?

Approved checks against each country’s published guidelines and flags anything risky, but final acceptance is always the issuing authority’s decision.

Do you store my photo?

No — it stays on your phone. It’s sent once to our AI service for the check and auto-deleted by the provider within 30 days; never used to train AI.

Is there a subscription?

No. You pay once — per photo or a 10-photo family pack.

Can I use a photo someone else took?

Yes — pick it from your library instead of using the camera.

A note on limits: compliance checking is conservative — borderline photos are flagged for retake rather than passed. It needs an internet connection for the one-time check (everything else works offline), and launches with ~12 countries/document types, expanding over time. Approved meets published country guidelines; final acceptance is at the issuing authority’s discretion.
